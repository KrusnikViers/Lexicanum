===Etymology 1===
From {{inh|en|enm|schep}}, {{m|enm|schepe}}, from Anglian {{inh|en|ang|sċēp}}, a variant of {{m|ang|sċēap}}, from {{inh|en|gmw-pro|*skāp}}, from {{inh|en|gem-pro|*skēpą}} (compare {{cog|fy|skiep}}, {{cog|frr|schäip}}, {{cog|nl|schaap}}, {{cog|de|Schaf}}), beside {{m|gem-pro|*keppô}} (compare {{cog|non|kjappi|t=buck}}, dialectal {{cog|de|Kippe|t=newborn calf}}), of unknown origin. Perhaps from the same {{der|en|xsc|-}} word (compare {{cog|os|цӕу|t=goat}}, {{cog|fa|چپش|t=yearling goat|tr=čapiš}})<ref>Vladimir Orel, ''A Handbook of Germanic Etymology'', s.vv. "*keppōn", "*skēpan" (Leiden: Brill, 2003), 213, 340</ref> which was borrowed into {{cog|sq|-}} as {{m|sq|cjap}}, {{m|sq|sqap|t=buck}} and into Slavic (compare {{cog|pl|cap}}). After Kroonen, ''*skēpą'' is instead from the root of {{cog|gem-pro|*skabaną|t=to scratch}} via {{w|Kluge's law}}.<ref>Guus Kroonen (2011), ''The Proto-Germanic ''n-''stems: a study in diachronic morphophonology'' [http://books.google.de/books?id=9stMq8rRnCMC], Rodopi, {{ISBN|9042032936}}.</ref>

====Noun====
{{en-noun|~|sheep|sheeps|pl3qual=nonstandard, mostly humorous}}

# {{lb|en|countable}} A [[woolly]] [[ruminant]] of the genus ''[[Ovis]]''.
# {{lb|en|countable|narrowly|}} A member of the [[domestic]] species ''[[Ovis aries]]'', the most well-known species of ''[[Ovis]]''.
# {{lb|en|countable}} A timid, shy person who is easily led by others.
#: {{synonyms|en|lamb|ovine|Thesaurus:shy person}}
# {{lb|en|countable|chiefly|Christianity|chiefly plural}} A [[religious]] [[adherent]], a member of a [[congregation]] or religious [[community]] (compare {{m|en|flock}}).
#* '''1990''', Dave Mustaine, "Holy Wars... The Punishment Due", Megadeth, ''Rust in Peace''.
#*: {{quote|en|And fools like me, who cross the sea and come to foreign lands / Ask the '''sheep''', for their beliefs do you kill on God's command?}}
# {{lb|en|uncountable}} [[sheepskin|Sheepskin]] [[leather]].
# {{lb|en|countable|speech recognition}} A person who is easily understood by a [[speech recognition]] system; contrasted with {{m|en|goat}}.
